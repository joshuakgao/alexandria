# GPIC: A Giant Permissive Image Corpus for Visual Generation

![[2026-gpic-thumbnail.png]]

## Research gap

Large-scale visual generative models require massive, diverse training datasets, but existing benchmarks fail to satisfy all requirements simultaneously. ImageNet-1K is class-conditional and FID-saturated after a decade of hill-climbing. Industrial datasets are proprietary or distributed as unstable URL indices subject to link rot. No existing benchmark is simultaneously **permissive** (commercially usable), **stable** (centrally hosted, frozen), **large** (100M+ images with text captions), and **accessible** (easy to download without crawling infrastructure).

## Contributions

- **GPIC dataset**: 100M training, 200K validation, and 1M test image-text pairs (~28 trillion pixels), all permissively licensed (CC BY, CC0, Public Domain), centrally hosted on Hugging Face as 8,000 balanced shards (12.9TB total).
- **Construction pipeline**: Licensed image sourcing from Flickr/Wikimedia, VLM-based quality and safety filtering, SSCD-based copy-detection deduplication with a conservative two-tier threshold, and multi-granularity captioning (tag/short/medium/long) via Qwen3-VL-4B.
- **Benchmarking protocol**: Evaluation using FD-DINOv2 (replacing saturated FID) against a held-out 1M test set, with oracle real-vs-real reference metrics and compliance guidelines discouraging DINOv2-aligned training objectives.
- **Reference baseline**: JiT-T2I (1.1B pixel-space flow matching model) trained on GPIC-Full for one epoch, achieving FD-DINOv2 of 76.25 at CFG 6.25.

## Method

1. **Source pool & licensing**: 110.6M images from Flickr (87.7%) and Wikimedia (12.3%) under permissive licenses. Provenance and attribution metadata retained for every image.
2. **Image filtering**: Resolution/aspect-ratio filters, VLM-based quality filtering (blur, under/overexposure), and safety filtering via Qwen3-VL-4B. Removes ~0.66% of the source pool.
3. **Deduplication**: SSCD copy-detection features with FAISS nearest-neighbor search. A two-tier rule removes high-confidence duplicates (similarity > 0.9625) and large near-copy clusters (≥5 connected images above 0.90). Power-law collision models on subsets predict removals before full-corpus pass. 101.3M images remain.
4. **Captioning**: Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct generates captions in four formats — tag (1%), short (45%), medium (45%), long (9%). Selected via a custom microbenchmark (1,520 images) evaluating quality, counting, spatial understanding, attribute binding, and OCR. ~1,500 H100 GPU-hours for full corpus.
5. **Splits & release**: Nested tiers — GPIC-Nano (1M), GPIC-Lite (10M), GPIC-Full (100M) — each preserving source and caption-type distributions. Released as frozen tar shards on Hugging Face.

## Datasets & evaluation

- **Evaluation metric**: FD-DINOv2 (Fréchet Distance over DINOv2 features) replaces FID/Inception. Shown to be unsaturated and better correlated with human judgment.
- **Protocol**: Generate 50K images from fixed test captions, compare against precomputed 1M test-set statistics. Oracle real-vs-real FD-DINOv2: 7.44 (Test-50K vs Test-1M).
- **Baseline results** (JiT-T2I, 1 epoch on GPIC-Full):

|-----|-----------|-----------|--------|---------|----------|
| 1.75 | 204.01 | 0.917 | 0.530 | 1.034 | 0.806 |
| 4.00 | 87.80 | 0.933 | 0.765 | 1.012 | 0.906 |
| 6.25 | 76.25 | 0.942 | 0.792 | 1.014 | 0.908 |

## Limitations

- Near-duplicates may remain despite deduplication efforts.
- Captions are synthetic (Qwen3-VL-4B) and may contain hallucinations or miss fine-grained details.
- Source images are biased toward Flickr/Wikimedia demographics and content distributions.
- Single-epoch baseline is not optimized — serves as a reference point, not a competitive model.
- Shares societal risks of large-scale image corpora: potential misuse for harmful generation, memorization, and bias amplification.

## Key takeaways

- The four-criteria framework (permissive, stable, large, accessible) provides a clear rubric for evaluating dataset suitability for modern generative modeling research.
- FD-DINOv2 is a meaningful replacement for saturated ImageNet FID — no current model beats the real-image oracle on GPIC, unlike ImageNet where several models already do.
- Conservative deduplication (guided by collision models) is preferable when permissive images are expensive to obtain — aggressive thresholding removes too many distinct images.
- Centrally hosting frozen shards eliminates link rot and dataset drift, enabling true reproducibility across labs and time.
