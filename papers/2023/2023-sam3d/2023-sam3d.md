# SAM3D: Segment Anything in 3D Scenes

![[2023-sam3d-thumbnail.png]]

## Research gap

SAM3D proposes a framework for lifting Segment Anything Model (SAM) segmentation results from 2D RGB images to 3D point clouds without requiring any training or finetuning. Given a 3D scene represented as a point cloud with posed RGB images, the method applies SAM to each RGB frame to generate 2D segmentation masks, projects these masks into 3D space, and then iteratively merges masks across frames using a bidirectional merging approach. The masks are merged in a bottom-up fashion to produce consistent segmentation labels for the entire 3D scene. The final step optionally ensembles the SAM3D results with over-segmentation based on geometric information to achieve finer-grained masks. The approach is evaluated qualitatively on ScanNet indoor scenes and demonstrates accurate, fine-grained 3D instance segmentation without any training on 3D data.

## Contributions

- A zero-shot framework that adapts SAM (trained on 2D RGB) to 3D scene segmentation via projection and merging
- Bidirectional merging strategy to resolve conflicts when merging masks from overlapping frames
- Bottom-up hierarchical merging approach to efficiently merge masks across hundreds or thousands of frames
- Ensemble method combining semantic/edge information from SAM with geometric information from over-segmentation
- Demonstration that SAM alone can produce high-quality, sometimes finer-grained masks than ground-truth annotations

## Method

The method operates in four main stages:

1. **Single-frame 3D masks**: Apply SAM automatic mask generation on RGB images. For pixels covered by multiple SAM masks (nested masks at different granularities), assign the mask ID with highest predicted IoU. Project 2D pixel coordinates to 3D using camera calibration matrices and depth information via the standard projection formula. Apply grid pooling to downsample the point cloud.

2. **Bidirectional merging**: Given two adjacent point cloud frames X¹ and X², compute correspondence mapping between points. For each mask ID m in X¹, find overlapping mask IDs n in X² using an overlap threshold (δ=0.5). Merge masks bidirectionally to resolve conflicts and ensure consistent labeling.

3. **Bottom-up hierarchical merging**: Process K frames in ⌈log₂K⌉ merging stages. At each stage, merge pairs of adjacent point clouds, gradually expanding from local frames to the full scene. Apply grid pooling after each merging step.

4. **Mask ensemble**: Optionally merge SAM3D results with over-segmentation masks computed via normal-based graph cuts on geometric information, creating a refined output combining semantic and geometric cues.

## Datasets & evaluation

Qualitative results on ScanNet indoor scenes show that SAM3D:
- Generates accurate, fine-grained instance segmentation masks across complex indoor environments
- Produces masks at multiple scales, capturing objects, parts, and fine details
- Sometimes segments details (e.g., paintings on walls) not present in ground-truth annotations
- Demonstrates that semantic and edge information from RGB is complementary to geometric-only approaches
- Achieves reasonable segmentation quality without any training or adaptation on 3D data

## Limitations

- Evaluation is purely qualitative on ScanNet; no quantitative benchmarks comparing to supervised 3D segmentation methods or prior zero-shot approaches
- The bidirectional merging and grid pooling introduce hyperparameters (overlap threshold δ=0.5, pooling resolution) whose sensitivity is not analyzed
- Method assumes access to accurate camera poses and depth; sensitivity to pose/depth errors unknown
- Computational cost of merging hundreds/thousands of frames not discussed
- Extension to open-vocabulary 3D segmentation and interactive prompting (leveraging SAM's prompt capabilities) noted as future work
- No analysis of failure cases or discussion of which scene characteristics lead to poor segmentation

## Key takeaways

SAM3D builds directly on the Segment Anything Model (SAM), which achieved state-of-the-art fine-grained 2D segmentation through prompt engineering and natural image training at scale. Prior work on 3D segmentation either trained directly on point clouds (PointNet, PointNet++, PointCNN) or performed multi-view fusion from RGB-D. SAD extended SAM to depth maps but still operated on 2D-like inputs. SAM3D's contribution is showing that 2D-to-3D projection with careful merging preserves and even enhances the segmentation quality of 2D foundation models.
