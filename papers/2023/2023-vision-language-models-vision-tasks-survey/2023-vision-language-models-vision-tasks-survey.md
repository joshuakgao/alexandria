# Vision-Language Models for Vision Tasks: A Survey

![[2023-vision-language-models-vision-tasks-survey-thumbnail.png]]

## Research gap

This comprehensive survey systematically reviews Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and their applications to visual recognition tasks. VLMs learn rich vision-language correlations from web-scale image-text pairs, enabling zero-shot predictions on diverse visual recognition tasks with a single model. The paper covers VLM foundations (network architectures, pre-training objectives, downstream tasks), widely-adopted datasets, three main categories of methods (pre-training, transfer learning, and knowledge distillation), and provides extensive benchmarking and analysis. The survey consolidates the landscape of VLM research, discussing research challenges and identifying future directions for the field.

## Contributions

- Comprehensive taxonomy of VLM architectures, training objectives, and applications
- Systematic categorization of VLM pre-training methods, transfer learning approaches, and knowledge distillation techniques
- Extensive benchmarking analysis comparing different VLM families and their performance across tasks
- Identification of research challenges and future directions for VLM development

## Method

Survey methodology involves literature review, categorization, and benchmarking analysis of existing VLM approaches across different task domains and training paradigms.

## Datasets & evaluation

Provides comprehensive analysis of how different VLM designs and training approaches perform across various downstream tasks, identifying strengths and weaknesses of different approaches.

## Limitations

Survey reflects knowledge up to early 2023; rapid pace of VLM development means some newer methods may not be covered. Evaluation metrics and benchmarks may themselves have limitations in fairly assessing multimodal understanding.

---

## Key takeaways

Synthesizes and organizes existing VLM literature into coherent categories, building on foundational work like CLIP while examining more recent architectures and training strategies.
