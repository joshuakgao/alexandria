# World Models

![[2018-world-models-thumbnail.png]]

## Research gap
Traditional model-free RL methods use small neural networks due to the credit assignment problem, limiting representational capacity. Learning directly from high-dimensional pixel observations in complex environments is sample-inefficient, and agents lack an internal model of the world that would allow them to plan or simulate outcomes without interacting with the real environment.

## Contributions
- A modular agent architecture (V-M-C) that separates vision (VAE), memory/prediction (MDN-RNN), and control into distinct components, allowing the world model to absorb most of the complexity while keeping the controller minimal (867 parameters).
- First known solution to solve the CarRacing-v0 environment (score 906 ± 21 over 100 trials), using learned spatial-temporal representations from raw pixels.
- Demonstration that an agent can be trained entirely inside its own hallucinated dream environment (VizDoom Take Cover) and transfer the learned policy back to the real environment, surpassing the gym leaderboard.
- Analysis of adversarial exploitation of learned world models and a temperature-based mechanism to control the trade-off between realism and exploitability in dream training.

## Method
The agent consists of three components: (1) **V (Vision)** — a convolutional VAE that compresses each observed frame into a latent vector z; (2) **M (Memory)** — a MDN-RNN that predicts the probability distribution of the next latent vector P(z_{t+1} | a_t, z_t, h_t) as a mixture of Gaussians, capturing stochastic environment dynamics; (3) **C (Controller)** — a single linear layer mapping the concatenation of z_t and h_t to actions. V and M are trained via backpropagation on data collected from random rollouts. C is trained using CMA-ES (Covariance-Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy) to maximize cumulative reward either in the real environment or inside the dream environment generated by M. For dream training, M also predicts a done signal, creating a complete virtual environment in latent space. A temperature parameter τ controls sampling stochasticity during dream generation, trading off between realism and robustness to model exploitation.

## Datasets & evaluation
**CarRacing-v0**: Randomly generated tracks; agent controls steering, acceleration, brake. V model trained on 10,000 random rollouts. Full world model achieves 906 ± 21 (previous best: 838 ± 11). V-only agent scores 632 ± 251, demonstrating the value of temporal prediction from M. **VizDoom Take Cover**: Agent must dodge fireballs to survive. Trained entirely in dream environment, achieves ~1100 time steps in the real environment (threshold: 750). Training in higher-temperature (more stochastic) dreams produces more robust policies that transfer better to reality.

## Limitations
- The VAE encodes task-irrelevant features (e.g., wall textures) while missing task-relevant ones (e.g., road tiles), since unsupervised learning cannot know what matters for the task.
- The LSTM-based world model has limited memory capacity and suffers from catastrophic forgetting, constraining it to relatively simple environments.
- The controller can discover adversarial policies that exploit imperfections in the world model (e.g., making fireballs disappear), requiring careful temperature tuning.
- Step-by-step simulation lacks hierarchical planning or abstract reasoning — the agent cannot ignore irrelevant spatial-temporal details.
- Only demonstrated on relatively simple 2D environments; scaling to complex 3D worlds with richer dynamics remains open.

## Key takeaways
- Separating representation learning (world model) from policy learning (controller) enables training compact policies that solve tasks from raw pixels — the controller needs only 867 parameters because the world model handles perceptual and predictive complexity.
- Agents can learn entirely inside hallucinated dream environments and transfer to reality, offering massive computational savings over real-environment training and potential for sim-to-real transfer.
- Stochastic world models (MDN-RNN with adjustable temperature) are critical for preventing adversarial exploitation — deterministic models suffer mode collapse and produce policies that fail in reality.
- The framework anticipates key ideas in later world model research: learned latent dynamics for planning, dream-based training, and the tension between model fidelity and policy robustness.
